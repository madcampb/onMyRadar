OCTOBER 11, 2025: for the chase

 

Wednesday, October 15

Listening Party!

DCTV

87 Lafayette Street

7:30pm ($16 + $1.50 fees, tix here) 

 

I’m intrigued by this — a quarterly series showcasing audio documentaries and sound art. The work featured in this event will all focus on the theme of “long distance.” 

 

Set Your Sights: New York Review of Books and Paula Cooper Gallery fall mixer

Paula Cooper Gallery

534 West 21st Street

6pm - 8pm (free)

 

There will be readings from Sophie Calle’s “On The Hunt” exhibition and vintage NYRB personals with an all star lineup including my faves Vivian Gornick, Katie Kitamura, and Leanne Shapton. From the event description: “Refreshments will be served; numbers may be exchanged. All are welcome—especially the single and the searching. Free. Come for the chase.”

 

Friday - Sunday, October 17 - 19

Open House New York

Various locations

All weekend (mostly free)

 

It’s that time of year again! Open House New York takes place this weekend. I never plan far enough in advance to get into the buzzier places but it’s just as well — it's hard to go wrong here.

 

Saturday, October 18

The Great Community Bake Sale

WSA

180 Maiden Lane

11am - 3pm (free admission, although they are also selling $14 presale vouchers that can be exchanged for a pastry of your choosing)

 

Pastry chef and cookbook author Natasha Pickowicz is teaming up with a few other food people to throw a huge bake sale with proceeds going to Heart of Dinner, Make the Road New York, One Love Community Fridge, and Lenox Hill Neighborhood House. See the (impressive) lineup of participants here.

 

Saturday - Sunday October 18-19

Ragas Live Festival

Pioneer Works

159 Pioneer Street

7pm - 7pm ($75 + $11.81 fees, tickets here)

 

Do I know what I’m in for? No. Am I slightly concerned about sleeping over at Pioneer Works? Yes. My dear friend NG wrote, “It’s my third year going and it’s truly my favorite music festival…I can say with certainty that you won’t be disappointed. It’s very weird, fun, and vaguely psychedelic.” Sometimes you just need a trusted friend to push you out of your comfort zone! Will report back on how this mid-30s body responds. 

 

Sunday October 19

US Launch of Charlie Porter’s Nova Scotia House

Readings at Parkside

317 East Houston Street

4pm (free)

 

I am extremely pumped for this one. I knew within the first few pages of Nova Scotia House that it would destroy me, and I was not wrong. Reading this over a series of jetlaggy early mornings was such a gift — it’s so so tender and devastating but also incredibly inspiring. Highly recommend! The book is officially out on October 21st, but copies are already available at Three Lives & Company. 

 

SEE ALSO: Charlie Porter at Hauser & Wirth’s Ursula on October 21 from 6-8pm (free)
