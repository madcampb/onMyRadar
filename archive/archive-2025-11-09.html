NOVEMBER 9, 2025: hitting the nail on the head

 

Tuesday, November 11

BOOK TALK: Olivia Laing on “The Silver Book”

Center for Fiction (virtual only!)

7pm - 8:15pm EST (tickets are $5 for the talk, $27 for talk + book)

 

I’ve only read Olivia Laing’s nonfiction, so it’ll be interesting to see where they go with historical fiction. The book is set in the world of 1970s Italian cinema, centered around real-life costume designer Danilo Donati, and described as “lushly atmospheric and politically charged.” What more do you need? 

 

While digging into Donati online, I found this website (lol at the tagline) with a bunch of screenshots of his costuming work, if you’d like to see.

 

DOC SCREENING: Orwell: 2 + 2 = 5 + talk with director Raoul Peck 

Brooklyn Public Library (Central Branch, Dweck Center)

10 Grand Army Plaza

6:30pm - 9pm (free // sold out)

 

I’m relisting this one again even though it’s now sold out — the doc is still showing at IFC (although the hours are odd). It also looks like it’ll be streaming on Amazon Prime fairly soon. Trailer here. See the rest of the BPL’s LitFilm festival lineup here. 

 

Wednesday, November 12

various locations / times

DOC NYC kicks off! Here are just a few films that caught my eye: Seeds, Plan C for Civilization, Paul, Mr. Nobody against Putin, Merchants of Joy, and Suburban Fury.

 

Thursday, November 13

BOOK SIGNING: Leanne Shapton, “In Cars: On Diana”

Dashwood Books

33 Bond Street

6pm - 8pm (free)

 

I somehow completely missed that Leanne Shapton had come out with this book in 2022 (I think this is a second edition / re-release). Shapton’s “Swimming Studies” made a huge impression on me in my 20s, so I’m curious about this book, which uses black and white watercolors of Princess Diana to reframe celebrity and identity. 

 

Friday - Saturday, November 14 - 15

DANCE: Times Four / David Gordon: 1975/2025

New York Live Arts (offsite)

541 Broadway, 2nd floor

7pm (tickets are $33.85)

 

I’m relisting this one again — tickets are selling out fast! The performance, which is an excavation and recreation of a 50-year-old dance that was only partially documented, stayed with me for days afterwards. I was going to link to the NYT writeup, but I actually think it's best to go in completely cold. 

 

A handout at the end of the performance included these two reviews, which really hit the nail on the head: “It was like watching two strands of hair when the comb goes through it” and “It was possible to be torn between thinking you’d die if it didn’t end soon and thinking you’d never tire of it.”

 

Saturday, November 15

DOC SCREENING: US premiere of “Louise Bourgeois: The Rage to Understand”

Hauser and Wirth 

443 West 18th Street 

3pm - 4pm (free with registration // sold out. Join the waitlist here or email me if you’re interested! I have two extra tix)

 

Hauser and Wirth is screening this new documentary to mark the opening of the Louise Bourgeois show. I’m excited to learn more — while Bourgeois’ work is inescapable, I actually know very little about her. 

 

I have two extra tickets if you’d like to come! It also looks like there’s a second screening at the close of the show on Saturday, January 24 at 3pm — nab your tickets now!
