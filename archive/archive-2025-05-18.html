MAY 18, 2025: let's get into it

 

TALKS:

 

Monday, May 19

Research Out Loud: The Lives of Objects 2

Metropolitan Museum of Art

1000 Fifth Avenue, Bonnie J. Sacerdote Lecture Hall or online

10:30am – 12:30pm (free, webinar registration here)

 

The Met fellows are giving presentations all month in a series called "Research Out Loud." It doesn’t get much better than a title like this: “A Stinky Challenge: Research, Study, and Removal of Deodorant Stains from a Geoffrey Beene Late-1960s Black Ensemble.” More details on the other lectures here (skip to page 10 for Monday's talk).

 

A completely unnecessary aside: If you're looking for a small jolt of surprise and wonder, I highly recommend running ebay + etsy searches on old/defunct brands a la Geoffrey Beene every now and again. It’s not about buying, it’s about the thrill of looking. (If this resonates in any way, please tell me your vintage search terms. I am dying to know).

Center for Fiction

15 Lafayette Ave

7pm - 8:15pm ($10 for in-person ticket, $5 for livestream - tickets here)

[reupping from last week]

I have admittedly only read parts of "Let the Record Show: A Political History of ACT UP New York, 1987–1993" -- a fact I see as my own failing. I was listening to this 18-minute conversation the other day and was struck all over again by Schulman's clear-eyed pragmatism.
