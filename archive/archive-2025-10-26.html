OCTOBER 26, 2025: hidden pockets of interest

 

Monday, November 3

TOUR: The Gay Harlem Renaissance

New York Historical Society

170 Central Park West

11am ($35, tix here)

 

At this point I should really just get the NYHS membership. Two of the curators will be leading a tour of the exhibition, which “explores the queer mentorship and gay-inclusive salons and friendship circles that helped sustain the Harlem Renaissance"  and "provides a sweeping portrait of Harlem after the First World War.” The exhibition is on until March 8, 2026.

Wednesday, November 5

TALK: Peter Hujar’s New York

Lincoln Center (Elinor Bunin Munroe Film Center Amphitheater)

144 West 65th Street

5pm (free, tickets will be available on a first-come, first-served basis one hour before the event)

 

Join director Ira Sachs ahead of the release of Peter Hujar’s Day for a “deep-dive discussion into the inspiration behind the film and the connection between Peter Hujar and his deeply felt legacy in New York City.” There will be a reception to follow to celebrate the special exhibition of rare contact sheets and prints of Hujar’s work at Walter Reade Theater.

Friday - Saturday, November 7 - 8

BOOK SALE: Municipal Archives and Library Book Sale

Department of Records and Information Services / Surrogate’s Courthouse

31 Chambers Street 

10am - 4pm both days (free)

 

The Municipal Archives and Library is getting rid of a ton of its books! From the event listing: “Whether you like history, politics, culture, theater, art, food, or iconic landmarks, there are books for everyone who loves New York City. Hundreds of books, all at great prices.”

 

Friday, November 7

LIVE MUSIC: Saha Gnawa

Whitney Museum of Art

99 Gansevoort St

6:30, 8:30 (two sets, free admission on Fridays - reserve tix here)

 

I very spontaneously saw this band perform a few months ago and had a ball. The sound is rooted in Gnawa music, but then they collaborate with musicians like the guitarist from Wilco. Anyway, they just released their first album so you can listen for yourself. This is part of the Durations Festival, and the show takes place during the Whitney's free admission window on Fridays (5-10pm) — I highly recommend going and experiencing their music live. 

 

LOOKING AHEAD:

 

Monday, November 17

Simon Raymonde on In One Ear: Cocteau Twins, Ivor Raymonde and Me

Parkside Lounge

317 East Houston Street

7pm - 9pm (doors at 6:30. free!)

 

Clear your calendars! This is the kinda shit that makes me want to twirl around with my arms extended and exclaim how lucky we are to be living in this time/place. Simon Raymonde of Cocteau Twins will be reading from and talking about his memoir. Cocteau Twins exists on such a mystical plane in my mind that it never occurred to me I could engage with the band/its members in real life??? I went to the Readings at Parkside event with Charlie Porter a few weeks ago and felt like I stumbled on an established world and network (can’t remember the last time I was in the same room with so many lesbian artists) so I’m excited to head back! 

 

And sorry not sorry this newsletter is basically my diary now and I simply must share: Someone I know spent decades mistakenly thinking that Jean Cocteau was the singer of Cocteau Twins, hahahahaha. As a Certified Music Dummy myself, I’m laughing all over again just remembering this lil nugget of delightful information.

BONUS CATNIP:

 

UNTIL NOVEMBER 14

If you are into craft and looking at beautiful things, go see this show, Echoes from Upstairs. Some context: You have to email Lauren Williams at hello@studiosolenne.com and make an appointment. I showed up not knowing what to expect (clearly a situation I put myself in frequently), and had to play it cool as it dawned on me that I was ringing the doorbell of a brownstone overlooking Cobble Hill Park and that this stranger was letting me into her actual house. The crown molding alone is breathtaking but then you get to see stuff like THIS.
