OCTOBER 19, 2025: extremely up my alley

 

Wednesday, October 22 - Sunday, October 26

THEATER: Lacrima

BAM Harvey Theater

651 Fulton Street

Various times (cheapest tix start at $46 including fees on Oct 25, other dates start at $66 and $75 including fees)

 

The premise of this play is extremely up my alley: A princess announces her engagement and a Parisian haute couture fashion house is commissioned to make the bridal dress. “Sworn to complete secrecy, craftspeople around the world embark on eight months of intricate labor … As these men and women all but obliterate themselves in pursuit of perfection, LACRIMA invites us into the space between that which destroys us and that which gives our lives purpose.”

 

Wednesday, October 22

DOCUMENTARY: Mistress Dispeller + Q&A with director Elizabeth Lo

IFC Center

323 6th Avenue

6:35pm ($18 + $1.95 fees, tickets here. Other showtimes available on Oct 22 + Oct 23)

 

I saw this last year at DOC NYC, and have thought about it repeatedly since. It follows a wife who, upon finding out that her husband is cheating on her, hires someone to effectively break up the affair. Jiayang Fan wrote about the practice of mistress dispelling in China for the New Yorker back in 2017 (the article gets into various practitioners and their different methods, if you’d like a primer). 

 

There are a few showtimes on October 22 and 23, but Elizabeth Lo is an incredible speaker and the backstory of how the film was made is pretty fascinating, so Wednesday’s Q&A should be good.  

 

Thursday, October 23

BOOK TALK: Arabelle Sicardi on her new book, House of Beauty

Books are Magic

122 Montague Street

7pm - 8pm ($10 ticket includes entry + $10 gift card, or $35 ticket includes entry + book, see here)

 

I think I first started following Arabelle Sicardi during the blogging era?? (A seminal time in my life, tbh). I totally forgot about them since I deleted my Twitter account, so I was pleasantly surprised to see this event come up on the Books are Magic events page.

 

Here’s a snippet from the book blurb: “Arabelle explores how beauty myths are crafted, sold, and weaponized … Follow alongside Arabelle as they trace the global trail of the shimmering mica in your beauty products … or journey into the past to unearth the sinister connection between fragrance and fascism.”

 

Friday October 24

SCREENING: The Phantom Carriage, with live score

The New School, John L. Tishman Auditorium

63 5th Avenue

7:30pm - 9:30pm (free, register here)

 

A thing that I accept is a me problem: the mere thought of Halloween induces an intense wave of existential weariness that I can't quite explain. A not-so-scary silent horror film screening is about as much as I could possibly muster for this holiday.
