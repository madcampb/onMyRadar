SEPTEMBER 28, 2025: ~ we're baaack!!!

 

Sunday, September 28

TALK: Stealing Time with Kent Jones, Kelly Reichardt, and Lucio Castro

David Rubenstein Atrium

1887 Broadway

5:15pm - 6:15pm ($20 + $3 fees, tickets here)

 

The New York Film Festival is on! Most of the screenings are already sold out, but there are still tickets to this talk this evening with three filmmakers who have made work “following artists whose efforts to make time and space for their creative passions are thwarted or frustrated by the grind of the everyday.” I’m particularly excited to hear from Kelly Reichardt, whose new film, “The Mastermind,” seems like a departure from her previous work?

 

SEE ALSO: Claire Denis and Barry Jenkins in conversation on Sunday, Oct 5 at 4pm ($23).

 

Monday, September 29

TALK: Ephemera and Movement Politics

School of Radical Attention

55 Washington Street Ste 736 (Brooklyn)

6:30pm - 8:00pm ($15 + $2.85 fees, tickets here)

 

From the page description: “Join us for a panel talk on the ongoing history of zines, posters, and ephemera in movement politics, with speakers Daylon Orr of Fugitive Materials and Jenna Freedman, Director of the Barnard Zine Library.” 

 

Thursday, October 2

SCREENING: Amplifying Voices from the Global South

SVA Theater

333 West 23rd Street

Doors at 6:30pm, screenings start at 7:15pm ($15 + $2.85 fees, tickets here)

 

Video Consortium is partnering with Al Jazeera English to screen four short docs that center around climate change and migration. 

 

Saturday, October 4

TALK: Tehching Hsieh on Lifeworks 1978-1999 with Adrian Heathfield, Humberto Moro, with Linda Montano

Dia Beacon

3 Beekman Street (Beacon, NY)

2pm (free, with museum admission)

 

Ok this is MAJOR and something I've been looking forward to for 1.5 years now : Tehching Hsieh’s first-ever retrospective opens at Dia Beacon, and he’ll be giving a free talk on opening day. If you’re unfamiliar with his work, there’s wiki and this, one of the more comprehensive/satisfying overviews online. 

 

Funny story: I’ve been fascinated by this man’s work for decades now, to the point where I set up a google alert for his name some years ago. Thanks to that google alert, I attended a talk he gave at the New York Studio School last year. I ended up posting about it online, and a high school classmate who I haven’t seen or talked to since graduation responded, writing, “That’s my uncle!”
