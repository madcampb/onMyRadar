NOVEMBER 16, 2025: unparalleled eavesdropping opportunities

 

Monday, November 17

BOOK TALK: Simon Raymonde on “In One Ear: Cocteau Twins, Ivor Raymonde and Me”

Parkside Lounge

317 East Houston Street

7pm - 9pm (doors at 6:30, free!)

 

I already enthused about this one enough in previous weeks. Be there or be square!

 

Wednesday, November 19

THEATER: “Inter Alia” (NT Live recording) 

Symphony Space (Leonard Nimoy Thalia Theater)

2537 Broadway

7:30pm (tickets are $27 including fees)

 

I watched “Prima Facie,” also written by Suzie Miller, on Broadway in 2023. Someone’s phone went off during the climax, but Jodie Comer was excellent nonetheless. A friend caught “Inter Alia” in London this summer, and it was fun to compare notes without either of us having seen the other play (they’re both legal dramas grappling with sexual assault). Anyway, this is a good opportunity to experience "Inter Alia" on the cheap.

 

Saturday, November 22

NATURE: Foraging with “Wildman” Steve Brill

Central Park 

West 72nd Street entrance

10am - 2pm (suggested donation is $20, register online or call (914) 835-2153 at least 24 hours in advance)

 

A few friends have gone foraging with Steve, and I’ve been hankering to spend time outdoors before things get really grim this winter. Apparently this is a good time — American persimmons, tart crab apples, the pod of the Kentucky coffee tree, and various roots, herbs, and vegetables are in season!

 

SEE ALSO: Foraging in Prospect Park with Steve’s daughter Violet at 11am on November 29 (suggested donation is $25, register online or call (914) 727-2552 at least 24 hours in advance).

 

MUSIC: Jace Clayton’s The Julius Eastman Memorial Dinner

Roulette Intermedium

509 Atlantic Ave

8pm, doors at 7pm (advance tickets are $27.50 including fees, and $30 at the door)

 

I encountered Julius Eastman’s music earlier this year in a haunting show at 52 Walker that featured four player pianos. Jace Clayton will be riffing on Eastman’s compositions (as he did in his 2013 album “The Julius Eastman Memory Depot”) by adding digital layers and distortions.

 

The performance at Roulette will also be live streamed for free at 8pm.

 

Sunday, November 23

DOCUMENTARY: 50th anniversary of “Grey Gardens”

Maysles Documentary Center

343 Malcom X Boulevard

Screenings at 2pm, 4:30pm, and 7:30pm (general admission is $15)

 

“Grey Gardens” turns 50! The Maysles Center is celebrating with three screenings, along with bagels, Bellinis, and non-alcoholic drinks.

 

 

ART:

 

I am behind on shows, but wanted to flag:

 

The new Studio Museum of Harlem location has opened as of yesterday! 

 

and…Sotheby’s is now open in the Breuer Building! I stopped by the other day to check out the Leonard Lauder collection and was shocked to find a line around the block. It was madness inside, and a mistake to assume the visit would be akin to my previous jaunts to the building; the difference between a museum and an art emporium becomes apparent pretty immediately. Still, there are some amazing paintings that are worth seeing in person if you can make it before November 18th. The people watching and eavesdropping opportunities also make for an experience, and if you’re lucky, you’ll get to see two art handlers carry an enormous Agnes Martin painting across a packed gallery. Wild.

 

 

That’s it for this week! Unless I come across something incredible that’s worth mentioning, I’ll be skipping the week of Thanksgiving!
