OCTOBER 5, 2025: fungus and theremins

 

Sunday, October 5

EXPLORE: New York Mycological Society’s Fungus Festival

Randall’s Island Urban Farm

Wards Meadow Loop

11am-3pm (free)

 

I have been following the New York Mycological Society for a while now and only just learned that the composer John Cage played a role in starting up the present day group! I’m coming in late with this one but there’s still time to make it a fun day out — the program includes plenty of interesting talks, fun workshops (mushroom cultivation!), walks, and more. See the full program here along with the schedule. 

 

Monday, October 6

TALK: Tim Berners Lee: The Invention of the World Wide Web, with Camille Francois and….Al Gore?

92nd Street Y (Buttenwieser Hall)

1395 Lexington Ave

7:30pm ($30 for in-person tickets, $25 for streaming, tickets here)

 

Tim Berners Lee, the inventor of the world wide web, is out with a new book. I know we’ve reached a point where “the internet is bad” (and “the dating apps are bad”) = “water is wet.” Apparently Berners-Lee has some thoughts about fixing things, although, as this article gets into…it doesn’t seem all that promising. (One notable line: “Berners-Lee sipped lemonade and stared at a projected image of Joe Rogan.”)

 

SEE ALSO: Cory Doctorow on Enshittification. Doctorow’s October 9th talk with Lina Kahn at the Brooklyn Public Library has reached maximum capacity, but he’ll be back in NYC on October 29 — it’s still unclear how open that talk will be, as it’s part of a whole conference, but I will monitor! I'm sure he'll also show up on loads of podcasts as part of the book tour blast.

 

Friday, October 10

WATCH: Art21 at the Movies

Museum of Modern Art

11 West 53rd Street

Programs run from 2:30pm to 10pm ($17.85 for individual events, or $33.85 for the all day pass, tickets here)

 

I’m reupping this one from last week — I'm excited for the Sophie Calle screening although I'm now regretting not getting the whole day pass! Random aside: I visited one of my best friends recently, and she recounted how she told her partner she loved him just days after they first kissed. I laughed and laughed and thought, “I love a woman with no chill.” That's also how I feel about Sophie Calle. 

 

Saturday, October 11

TALKS: Triple Canopy Symposium

Roulette

509 Atlantic Ave

Programs run all day from 2pm onwards (free with RSVP)

 

Not gonna lie, I was bracing myself for incomprehensible art speak and event titles with endless /// \\\s in them when I came across this (the late 2000s and early 2010s still loom large in my mind, ok?). I was pleasantly surprised by the lineup — I’m particularly interested in the talk at 2pm with Andrea Long Chu on bad criticism, and the 8pm talk/improvisation about “bass in and beyond Black music.”

 

Sunday, October 12

EXPLORE: Pioneer Works Second Sundays

Pioneer Works 

159 Pioneer Street

Programs run from 12pm - 6pm (free with RSVP)

 

There’s plenty to explore here, from the New York Theremin Society’s workshop to a talk with Chris Kraus!

 

SEE ALSO: Chris Kraus and Natasha Stagg at McNally Jackson Seaport on October 13 ($5 tix here).
